Audio Representations

Contrastive language-audio alignment (CLAP) and self-supervised music understanding (MERT)

Two complementary approaches to learning what music 'means' to a neural network: CLAP aligns audio with natural-language descriptions through contrastive learning, while MERT learns musical structure from audio alone via masked acoustic modeling with dual teachers.

LLMs for Audio Effects

Two papers from the Sony AI / KAIST group on using language models to drive EQ, compression, and reverb: first by predicting parameters, then by calling tools

Why audio effects are a uniquely tractable target for LLMs, and how the Doh et al. team moves from parameter regression (LLM2Fx) to tool-calling chains with chain-of-thought planning (LLM2Fx-Tools).

What we read

ML papers about music: synthesis, transcription, generative models, anything with a loss function and an audio output. We work through one or two a week.

How we share

Each session gets interactive notes with visualizations and audio demos (and usually some PyTorch). The goal is real intuition, not just a summary of the abstract.

Who it's for

Researchers, engineers, musicians, students. Anyone who wants to actually dig into the papers rather than just hear about them. Show up, ask questions, bring coffee.
